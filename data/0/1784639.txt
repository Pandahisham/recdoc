Vaginal Mucormycosis: A Case Report

This is an open access article distributed under the Creative Commons Attribution License, which permits unrestricted use, distribution, and reproduction in any medium, provided the original work is properly cited.
Although Zygomycetes cause life-threatening, opportunistic infections in immunocompromised hosts, the first caseof vaginitis caused by Mucor species in a healthy woman is reported. Mucor vaginitis, which caused mild symptoms only, was refractory to conventional azole therapy and resistant to flucytosine. Cure was achieved with topicalamphotericin B.
