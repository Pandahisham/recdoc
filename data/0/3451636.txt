Headache attributed to unruptured saccular aneurysm, mimicking hemicrania continua

Unruptured cerebral arterialaneurysms most often remainasymptomatic, but they may causeheadache or other symptoms orsigns. We describe herewith a caseof headache attributed to an unrupturednternal carotid arteryaneurysm, clearly mimicking thephenotype of hemicrania continua.Potential pathophysiological explanationsand recommendations forrecognition of similar cases are discussed.
